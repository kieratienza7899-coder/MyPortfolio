Ethics in the Computing Industry

Learning Objectives

Students will be able to:

  • Define ethics in computing
  • Identify major ethical issues in the computing industry
  • Apply ethical thinking to simple real-world cases

    • What Is Computing Ethics?

    Computing ethics refers to moral principles that guide the design, development, and use of technology. Ethical decisions in computing matter because technology impacts privacy, security, fairness, and society.

    Key Ethical Issues

  • Privacy: Protecting personal and sensitive data
  • Intellectual Property: Respecting ownership of software and data
  • Security: Preventing misuse, breaches, and harm
  • Bias & Fairness: Avoiding discrimination in algorithms and AI
  • Professional Responsibility: Acting honestly and in the public interest

    • Example Ethical Dilemmas

  • Follow professional codes (ACM / IEEE)
  • Consider consequences of technical decisions
  • Protect users and society
  • Speak up against unethical practices

    • Summary

    Ethics is a core responsibility in computing. Ethical professionals build trust, prevent harm, and create technology that benefits society.
