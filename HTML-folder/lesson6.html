Electronic Media

Definition

Electronic media refers to communication channels that use electronic technology to store, transmit, and receive information.

  • Examples: TV, radio, internet, digital platforms, social media.
  • Unlike print media, it is dynamic, interactive, and fast.

    • Types of Electronic Media

    Broadcasting Media

    Television, Radio

    Purpose/Use: Mass communication, news, entertainment

    Digital Media

    Internet, Websites, Social Media

    Purpose/Use:Online content, e-learning, marketing

    Interactive Media

    Video games, Apps, VR

    Purpose/Use:User engagement, training, simulations

    Advantages

  • Fast and wide-reaching
  • Interactive and engaging
  • Multimedia capability (text, audio, video)
  • Can store and retrieve information easily

    • Disadvantages

  • Can spread misinformation quickly
  • Privacy and security concerns
  • Digital divide may exclude some audiences

    • Applications

  • Education (e-learning platforms)
  • Entertainment (TV shows, streaming, gaming)
  • Marketing and advertising
  • News and information dissemination

    • Summary

    Electronic media uses technology to communicate information quickly quickly and widely. It can be broadcast, digital, or interactive, and has become central in education, entertainment, and business.
